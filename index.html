Projects & designs

🦿
Project 01 · CUBEC Conference Presentation 2025
StepUp — Foot Drop Brace
Assistive TechCUBEC 2025

Presented at the Canadian Undergraduate Biomedical Engineering Conference (CUBEC) in 2025. StepUp is a lightweight, comfortable foot brace for people with foot drop — a condition making it hard to lift the front of the foot. Unlike bulky conventional solutions, StepUp supports natural movement without constant manual adjustment.

💨
Project 02 · iBioMed Final Showcase
Smart Inhaler System
Health TechEntrepreneurship

Presented at the iBioMed Final Showcase. A smart inhaler concept with a wearable band that detects air pollutants in real time, a companion app for tracking and alerts, inhalation-force sensing to deliver medication only when effective, and visual cues for correct technique. Backed by a full lean canvas, SWOT, PESTLE, and market analysis.

🛵
Project 03
Scootable — Assistive Side Table
Assistive TechAccessibility

Designed for a multiple sclerosis patient with limited left-hand mobility. Scootable clamps onto a mobility scooter's armrest, telescopes out to a fold-flat table with a collapsible phone holder — enabling hands-free texting and calling, fully operable one-handed.

🛏️
Project 04
Overnight Ostomy Appliance
Medical DevicePatient Care

For a colostomy patient chronically sleep-deprived by overnight bag emptying. Our passive system connects the stoma bag via coated tubing to a bedside waste box — eliminating nighttime wake-ups entirely and restoring uninterrupted sleep.

🦴
Project 05
Custom Hip Implant
BiomaterialsOrthopaedics

A patient-specific femoral implant with retractable stem spikes for secure press-fit anchoring. The femoral head is coated with hydroxyapatite and fibroin nanoparticles for osseointegration, with a custom acetabular cup for patients with a shallow socket.

🐾
Project 06
Automated Pet Food Dispenser
AutomationEmbedded Systems

A smart dispenser for busy pet owners using a force sensor beneath the bowl to auto-dispense at user-set intervals — and notify owners if food goes uneaten, flagging potential eating abnormalities even when owners are away.
